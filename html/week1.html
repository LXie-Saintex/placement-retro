Things I enjoyed most this week: Type system, DOM, CSSOM and performance.

We had a very smooth introduction in my opinion. Craig asked about our preferences and listed out what we are looking to learn in the next 3 months. I said that I am more drawn to backend because I expect that there is more to learn in backend.

The daily tasks were interesting. Prior to this week, I did not have any knowledge of CI/CD, Kubernetes, Type system , Tokenisation & AST, and CSSOM. Everyday we read about the new topics, sometimes we prepare demos for demonstration. Then at the end-of-day catchup, we explain what they are, how do they work and why do we need them. For me, the hardest part was to understand the CI/CD process and Kubernetes. There are many introductory articles on those topics that give very similar explanations, and I found myself memorizing rather than understanding them. I might be able to explain CI/CD and Kubernetes only when I've started to use them.

Reading about type system, tokenisation and AST was fun. It helped me understood compiling a lot better. I'd like to use tools like Uglifier / terser to learn more about AST.
